What to Look For in a Health Education Provider

A qualified health education provider is a credentialed organization or individual that teaches evidence-based wellness, disease prevention, and health-literacy skills.

Look for CHES-certified instructors, written curricula, liability insurance, and transparent pricing.

The single most important credential is the CHES (Certified Health Education Specialist — a national credential issued by the National Commission for Health Education Credentialing after a 165-question exam). Advanced practitioners hold the MCHES. Both follow the seven core responsibilities defined by NCHEC, the national credentialing body (source: nchec.org).

The 7 health education skills are: assessing needs, planning programs, implementing programs, conducting evaluation and research, administration, serving as a resource person, and communication and advocacy. Any quality provider in Middleton should be able to explain how their work touches each one.

"Health education is any combination of planned learning experiences using evidence-based practices and/or sound theories that provide the opportunity to acquire knowledge, attitudes, and skills needed to adopt and maintain healthy behaviors."— Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, CDC National Health Education Standards
