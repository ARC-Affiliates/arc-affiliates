What Is an SEO Agency, Exactly?

An SEO agency is a company that improves a website's organic (unpaid) Google rankings using a mix of content, link-building, and technical work.

An SEO agency optimizes your website so it ranks higher on Google search results without paying for ads.

A legitimate SEO agency (search engine optimization agency — a firm that earns organic visibility for client websites) handles four broad service categories: keyword research, content production, link acquisition, and technical site audits. What Are the 4 Types of SEO?

The four types of SEO are on-page, off-page, technical, and local SEO. Each one targets a separate set of Google ranking signals.

On-page, off-page, technical, and local — these four categories cover every ranking factor Google uses.

1. On-Page SEO

This is everything on your own pages: title tags, headings, body content, internal links, image alt text, and keyword targeting. If a page doesn't clearly tell Google what it's about, no amount of backlinks will save it.

2. Off-Page SEO

Off-page SEO is mostly backlinks — when other reputable websites link to yours. Google treats links like votes of confidence. Brand mentions, PR placements, and citations on directories also count.

3. Technical SEO

This covers site speed, mobile usability, structured data (schema markup — code that tells search engines what your content means), crawl errors, and indexing. Google's own Core Web Vitals documentation outlines the technical thresholds (source: developers.google.com).

4. Local SEO

Local SEO targets the map pack and "near me" searches. It revolves around your Google Business Profile, NAP (name, address, phone) consistency, local citations, and reviews.

"Local search results favor the most relevant results for each search, and businesses with complete and accurate information are easier to match with the right searches."Google Search Central — support.google.com/business

Hiring Checklist: Verifying an SEO Agency

  1. Ask for 3 case studies with starting and current keyword rankings.
  2. Confirm they use Google Search Console data, not third-party-only reports.
  3. Verify month-to-month or 90-day contracts (avoid 12-month lock-ins).
  4. Request a sample technical audit before signing.
  5. Check that link-building uses real publications, not PBNs or fiverr links.
  6. Confirm they will give you full ownership of all content and assets.
  7. Ask which tools they use — Ahrefs, Semrush, and Screaming Frog are standard.

SEO Myths vs Facts

Myth: You can rank #1 in 30 days.

Fact: Competitive keywords take 6–12 months. Anyone promising 30-day rankings is selling spam tactics.

Myth: More keywords on a page equals higher rankings.

Fact: Keyword stuffing has been a ranking penalty since Google's 2011 Panda update.

Myth: SEO is a one-time setup.

Fact: Google updates its algorithm thousands of times per year. SEO is ongoing maintenance.

Myth: Local SEO doesn't matter if you serve a wide area.

Fact: 46% of all Google searches have local intent, per Google's own data.

What Credentials a Legitimate SEO Agency Should Have

Florida does not license SEO agencies, but credible firms typically hold:

  • Active Florida business registration (verifiable at sunbiz.org)
  • Google Partner status for any paid-search work (verifiable at google.com/partners)
  • General liability insurance ($1M minimum is industry standard)
  • Documented agency or contractor agreements with IP-ownership clauses
  • Certifications from HubSpot Academy, Semrush Academy, or Google Skillshop
