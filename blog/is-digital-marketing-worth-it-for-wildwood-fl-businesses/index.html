What Is the 70/20/10 Rule in Digital Marketing?

The 70/20/10 rule allocates 70% of your digital marketing budget to proven channels, 20% to emerging tactics, and 10% to experimental campaigns. Coca-Cola popularized the framework, and Google formalized it for paid media portfolios.

Spend 70% on what works, 20% on what's trending, and 10% on unproven bets.

According to ARC Affiliates, a $5,000/month Wildwood service business budget typically breaks down as $3,500 on Google Ads and SEO (proven), $1,000 on short-form video and email automation (emerging), and $500 on AI-driven tools or new ad formats (experimental). This structure protects cash flow while keeping the brand from falling behind when channels like TikTok Search or AI Overviews shift consumer behavior. The 10% experimental slice is where next year's 70% winners are found.

"Marketers who diversify across a portfolio of channels consistently outperform single-channel spenders, particularly when rebalancing quarterly." Harvard Business Review, hbr.org
