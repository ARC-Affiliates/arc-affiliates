What Should local service Contractors Verify Before Choosing an SEO Platform?

Legitimate SEO platforms disclose pricing, allow data export, and avoid long-term contracts.

Credentials & Transparency Markers

While SEO is not a state-licensed industry, legitimate providers should demonstrate:

  • Public pricing on the website (not "request a quote" only)
  • Month-to-month terms or clear cancellation rights
  • Full data export of content, citations, and analytics
  • Google Partner status or transparent methodology documentation (Google Search Central guidelines)
  • Compliance with FTC endorsement guidelines for testimonials (FTC guide)

local service SEO Platform Verification Checklist

  1. Confirm month-to-month billing with no early-termination fee.
  2. Request a sample location-page output to evaluate content quality.
  3. Verify you own the domain, content, and Google Business Profile.
  4. Check that citations are submitted under your business identity, not a proxy.
  5. Confirm exportable reporting (CSV / PDF) for rankings and traffic.
  6. Read at least 5 third-party reviews on G2, Capterra, or Trustpilot.
  7. Verify the platform supports your CMS (WordPress, Duda, Wix, etc.).

#Red flags to watch for

  • Demands a 12-month or longer contract with no opt-out clause.
  • Refuses to give you admin access to your own Google Business Profile.
  • Charges $5,000+ setup fees with no deliverable timeline.
  • Guarantees "#1 rankings" — a violation of Google's spam policies.
  • Won't disclose where citations and backlinks are placed.
  • Hosts your website on their proprietary platform with no export option.
